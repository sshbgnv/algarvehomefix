Why Proper AC Use (and Regular Servicing) Matters for Your Health

By Algarve Home Fix · 7 min read

Air conditioning in the Algarve isn't a luxury — for many homeowners, it's the difference between a comfortable summer and months of struggling to sleep, work, or simply relax indoors. Only 17% of homes in Portugal have AC, with the Algarve having the highest concentration in the country — which means many people here installed their system relatively recently, often without knowing how to get the health benefits right, or what happens when maintenance gets skipped.

Here's what's actually true about AC and your health — the good, the risks, and how to avoid the second one entirely.

The Real Health Benefits

A well-maintained air conditioning system does more than cool a room. It actively works on the quality of the air you breathe.

It filters what you breathe. As air passes through the system, the filter removes airborne allergens such as pet dander, dust, smoke particles, and in some cases bacteria. For anyone with allergies or asthma, this is a meaningful, daily difference — not a marginal one.

It controls humidity, which controls allergens. Mould and dust mites thrive above 60% relative humidity, while the recommended range for comfort and health sits between 40–60%. By keeping humidity in that range, your AC unit makes your home a much less hospitable environment for the things that trigger allergic reactions and respiratory irritation.

It protects you from heat stress. In high humidity, sweating becomes less effective at cooling the body, increasing the risk of dehydration and heat-related illness. During Algarve summers, this isn't theoretical — it's a real consideration for children, older residents, and anyone with existing health conditions.

It means you don't have to choose between fresh air and clean air. Many people in the Algarve still rely on open windows during the hottest months. The trade-off: open windows bring in outdoor pollen, dust, and humidity along with the breeze. A properly maintained AC system lets you keep windows closed during peak pollen or heat periods while still reducing indoor allergens and managing humidity — comfort and air quality together, not one instead of the other.

Where It Goes Wrong: The Maintenance Gap

Here's the part most homeowners don't hear until something smells off or a family member starts sneezing more indoors than outdoors.

A neglected unit becomes the problem it was meant to solve. If an air conditioner isn't properly maintained, it can become a source of allergens itself — mould can grow in the damp areas of the unit, such as the evaporator coils or drain pan, and those spores get distributed through your home every time the system runs.

Dirty filters don't just reduce comfort — they actively work against you. A clogged filter reduces airflow and lets allergens bypass filtration altogether, entering your living space instead of being trapped. At that point, you're running a system that uses electricity to circulate the very particles you wanted it to remove.

The risks are avoidable, and that's the point. The risks associated with air conditioning are almost entirely linked to poor maintenance or incorrect settings — not to air conditioning itself. Dry skin, throat irritation, and stuffiness are typically signs of a unit running too cold for too long, or running dirty. Both are fixable with the right settings and a regular service — not reasons to avoid AC altogether.

Getting the Settings Right

Two simple habits make a real difference between an AC system that helps your health and one that just cools the room:

  • Keep the temperature reasonable. 21–25°C is the recommended range — cold enough to be comfortable, not so cold that it stresses your respiratory system or dries out your skin and eyes.
  • Don't skip annual servicing. Most professionals recommend a tune-up at least once a year, ideally before the season when you'll rely on the system most. In the Algarve, that means before the summer heat arrives — not after the first time it stops cooling properly.

The Bottom Line

Air conditioning genuinely supports better indoor air quality, better sleep, and a lower health risk during hot, humid months — but only when it's serviced. An unmaintained unit doesn't just cool less efficiently; it can quietly turn into the source of the exact problems you installed it to avoid.

If your system hasn't been serviced in the last year, or you're not sure when it last was, that's the one thing worth checking before the next heatwave — not after.
